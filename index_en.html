APPROVAL TO PARTICIPATE IN A NON-BIOMEDICAL COVID-19 SOUND SCREENING TEST SURVEY

If you give consent, you agree to participate in this research study conducted by individual technology developer Ahmet Alptekin. Please read this entire page before continuing. The following information provides a summary of the research. Your participation in this research is voluntary and you can withdraw at any time by closing the browser or navigating to a different page.

PURPOSE

In this study, we will collect cough samples from COVID-19 patients and non-patients and try to differentiate the two groups using Artificial Intelligence.

OPERATING PROCEDURE

To participate, indicate information such as (1) your age, (2) your gender, (3) symptoms (fever, fatigue, sore throat, difficulty breathing, persistent pain or pressure in the chest, diarrhea, cough), whether you have been tested for COVID-19 and the result and agree to the Terms of Use. Filling and submitting the form takes less than 1 minute. You will record your cough using the recording feature on the website and the recording and your answers will be sent to us when you press the "SEND" button.

RISKS, PRECAUTIONS AND POTENTIAL PROBLEMS

Make sure no one else is within 5 meters of you when recording your cough. After submitting your registration, you should thoroughly disinfect your device and any other areas that may have been exposed to prevent any possibility of contamination.

POTENTIAL BENEFITS

By participating in this research work, you will contribute to the solution of a very important world problem. Finally, your participation will contribute to the development of an automated COVID-19 screening tool.

PRIVACY

No information will be kept from you other than cough records and answers to six questions. All data from the study will be stored on a secure server, where they will always be encrypted and accessible only to participating researchers. After the study is completed, a copy of the data will be securely stored so that we can use it for further relevant research, for example, should a pandemic occur in the future.
